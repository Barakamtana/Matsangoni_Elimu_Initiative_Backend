Who Are We

We are a passionate Community-Based Organization (CBO) based in Matsangoni Ward, Kilifi County, committed to elevating educational standards in our community. Concerned by persistently low academic performance and high dropout rates, we aspire to a future where see at least 80% of our students transition to higher education. We believe quality education is the cornerstone of progress and empowerment. We are determined to create opportunities for our kids to excel academically. However, we recognize that no single individual has all the expertise, resources, or know how to address these challenges alone. That’s why we are calling on individuals and organizations who share this vision to join us. Whether through volunteering skills, financial support, or in any manner they see fit—no matter how small—every effort counts. By partnering with us, you contribute to the UN Sustainable Development Goals 4 (Quality Education) and 10 (Reduced Inequalities). Together, through collective action and shared purpose, we can provide the resources, avenues, and hope needed to empower our students and transform our community.

Our Leadership

Mr. Mtana Mwahunga

Mr. Mtana, the Founder and Chairperson, MEI is a visionary leader driven by a profound belief that while opportunities may be limited, brilliance and talent are abundant. His unwavering commitment to unlocking the potential of every student in the community has been the cornerstone of this initiative. As the architect of this transformative mission, he inspires others with his dedication to creating pathways for success, regardless of the challenges faced. His leadership embodies hope, resilience, and a steadfast belief that through collaboration and shared purpose, this organization can break barriers, restore the glory of our schools, and nurture the untapped talent that exists within our youth. His passion and relentless pursuit of excellence set the foundation for the community’s brighter future.

CPA. Teddy Yanga

Mr. Teddy Yanga, who serves as our Secretary, holds a deep and personal connection to the mission. He attended the very schools that are now struggling, having completed his primary education at Maweni Primary School during a time when they were thriving. He has firsthand experience of the schools’ potential which fuels our unwavering belief that this CBO has the power to restore their lost glory. He is convinced that with the right resources, dedication, and collective effort, these schools can once again become beacons of academic excellence, empowering the next generation to succeed.
